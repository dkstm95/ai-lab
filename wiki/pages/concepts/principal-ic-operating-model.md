## Summary

A principal IC is most useful when they stay technically grounded while increasing organizational leverage. The role is less about owning every critical path directly and more about improving the decisions, priorities, and execution quality of many teams.

## Key Claims

- accepted: Principal ICs should reduce organizational dependency on themselves by moving from being essential on the critical path to being adjacent enough to guide, unblock, and multiply teams.
  source: raw/sources/advice-for-new-principal-tech-ics-fuller-notes.md
- accepted: Principal IC work can span product, design, engineering, science, quality, hiring, finance, and culture when the organization needs cross-functional judgment.
  source: raw/sources/advice-for-new-principal-tech-ics.md
- accepted: High-leverage principal work includes making important but under-valued work visible, valuable, and actionable to the organization.
  source: raw/sources/advice-for-new-principal-tech-ics-fuller-notes.md
- accepted: Principal ICs should distinguish owner, sponsor, and consultant roles so decision rights and support expectations are clear.
  source: raw/sources/advice-for-new-principal-tech-ics-fuller-notes.md
- accepted: Seniority gives casual comments, silence, and disagreement extra weight, so principal ICs should state intent, confidence, and reasoning explicitly.
  source: raw/sources/advice-for-new-principal-tech-ics-fuller-notes.md
- accepted: Principal ICs should make space for others to decide, speak, and receive credit instead of becoming the default answer for every problem.
  source: raw/sources/advice-for-new-principal-tech-ics-fuller-notes.md
- accepted: Breadth work can consume all available time, so principal ICs need protected thinking time and selective involvement.
  source: raw/sources/advice-for-new-principal-tech-ics.md
