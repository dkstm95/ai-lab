## Notes

This raw source records additional paraphrased review notes from the article. It is not a copy of the article.

- Principal ICs should reduce dependency on themselves by moving from being essential on the critical path to being adjacent enough to guide decisions and unblock teams.
- The role often becomes part-time product, design, engineering, science, quality, hiring, finance, and culture work when judgment is useful.
- High-leverage work can mean changing what the organization values, especially when important work is not naturally rewarded yet.
- Useful interventions can be small but high ROI: a timely review, context packet, sponsorship, correction, or question that changes a team's trajectory.
- Principal ICs should clarify whether they are acting as owner, sponsor, or consultant so teams know who decides and who supports.
- Seniority changes meeting dynamics: silence, casual comments, and disagreement can carry more weight than intended.
- Principal ICs should make room for others to decide, speak, and receive credit instead of filling every gap themselves.
- Review and promotion feedback should be calibrated to the level and person; low-quality or mismatched feedback can be declined or reframed.
- Principal ICs need protected thinking time because breadth work can otherwise expand into constant reviews, escalations, and meetings.
