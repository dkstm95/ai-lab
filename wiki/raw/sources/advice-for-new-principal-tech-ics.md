# Advice for New Principal Tech ICs

Author: Ziyou Yan
Publication date: October 2025
Accessed: 2026-06-18

## Notes

This raw source records a compact, paraphrased ingest note rather than a copy of the article.

- Principal ICs vary by strength: deep technical ownership, broad influence, pathfinding, or multi-org alignment.
- Work that made someone successful before principal level becomes only part of the job; leverage increasingly comes from vision, review, sponsorship, context, and multiplying others.
- Principal work spans product, design, engineering, science, quality, hiring, finance, and culture when judgment is useful.
- Communication, alignment, and convincing others to act are core parts of the role.
- A principal should focus on work that would not happen without them, especially at the intersection of durable organizational need and personal strengths.
- Scaling through coaching and delegation is central; the organization should increasingly make good decisions without depending on the principal as a bottleneck.
- Seniority carries implicit influence, so casual comments should be framed with context, uncertainty, and reasoning.
- Breadth roles require active time protection, deliberate thinking time, and clear charters such as owner, sponsor, and consultant.
- Long-term effectiveness includes peer support, continued learning, and attention to burnout risk.
